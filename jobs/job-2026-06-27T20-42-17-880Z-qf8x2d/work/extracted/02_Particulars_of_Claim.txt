IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUSTICE — TECHNOLOGY AND CONSTRUCTION COURT (TCC)
Claim No. HT-2025-000231
Meridian Retail Group plc (Claimant) v TechFlow Solutions Limited (Defendant)

PARTICULARS OF CLAIM — dated 5 June 2025

3. By a written Master Services Agreement dated 14 March 2024 ("the MSA"), TechFlow agreed to
design, build, configure and implement for Meridian a cloud-based inventory management and
electronic point-of-sale platform ("the Platform").

5. The total charges payable under the MSA were £2,400,000 excluding VAT, payable against milestones.

6. It was an express term of the MSA and the SOW that the go-live date for the Platform was fixed at
1 October 2024, and that time was of the essence in respect of that date.

7. At a meeting on or about 5 February 2024, prior to entering the MSA, TechFlow by its Sales
Director Mr Daniel Frost orally represented to Meridian that the Platform would reliably support at
least 10,000 concurrent transactions. The representation was false, the Platform being incapable of
supporting that volume, and Meridian entered the MSA in reliance upon it.

8. In breach of the MSA, TechFlow delivered the Platform late. The Platform did not go live until
18 November 2024, some seven weeks after the contractual go-live date.

9. Meridian did not at any time request any change to the agreed scope of the works. All delay was
caused by TechFlow's failure to allocate adequate and competent resources to the project.

10. On numerous occasions prior to go-live, Meridian warned TechFlow that the Platform was not ready
and requested that go-live be deferred. TechFlow ignored those warnings and proceeded to go-live on
18 November 2024.

11. Following go-live, the Platform was unavailable for more than 40% of trading hours during
November and December 2024, causing widespread till failures and an inability to process sales across
Meridian's store estate.

12. The Platform contained numerous defects, including critical (Severity 1) failures in the
stock-synchronisation module which caused stores to display and sell against incorrect stock figures,
such that the Platform was not of satisfactory quality and was not reasonably fit for its purpose.

13. Meridian did not accept the Platform or any part of it, and no acceptance or sign-off was given
by Meridian at any time.

14. TechFlow failed to provide adequate training to Meridian's staff in the use of the Platform.

15. By reason of the matters aforesaid Meridian has suffered loss and damage, namely (a) wasted
expenditure, being sums paid to TechFlow under the MSA, of £1,800,000; and (b) loss of profit during
the peak trading period of November and December 2024 of £4,200,000.
