POST OFFICE HORIZON IT INQUIRY

FIRST WITNESS STATEMENT OF RUTH SIMPSON

I, RUTH SIMPSON, will say as follows...

1. This statement is made to assist the Post Office Horizon IT Inquiry and relates to the RULE 9 request dated 5th May 2023, which relates to my experience at Marine Drive Post Office and my involvement in the litigation against Lee Castleton. At that time, I was responsible as a Sub-Postmistress for the Post Office, at First Lane, Hessle.

2. I confirm that I gave a statement on the 19th of October 2006 and attended at Court, as a witness, in proceedings to which that statement related, I gave evidence on behalf of the Post Office [LCAS0000121].

3. I confirm I have had the opportunity to consider the statement I made once again, and I confirm the content is a true account of my recollection, of the experience I had at Marine Drive Post Office. My recollection is limited to the

Yorkshire Bank PLC, progressing to the bank branch manager, a role I
remained in for 23 years. In 1999 I left the Yorkshire bank to be a Sub-
Postmistress for the Post Office. From the year 1999 to 2015, I worked as the
Sub-Postmistress, at First Lane Post Office, in Hessle, until my retirement in
2015.

6. During the year 2004, I was asked by a lady called Cath Oglesby, who was my
then line manager at the Post Office, to stand in as Sub-Postmistress, at Marine
Drive Post Office, following the suspension of the Postmaster there, Lee
Castleton. I was informed that the suspension was due to the discovery of
unauthorised losses.

7. I therefore ran the Marine Drive Post Office, between 23rd March 2004 and 21st
April 2004. Cath Oglesby was my line manager before this date and with whom
I had a good working relationship. Cath Oglesby remained my line manager
during the time at Marine Drive Post Office. I am unable to recall the frequency
of my communication with Cath Oglesby, before or during my time at Marine
Drive Post Office, due to the passage of time. I believe I would have provided
regular updates given the circumstances.

8. In relation to the weekly balances at the Post Office, whilst I was there, I can
only comment from the information in my statement made 19th October 2006,
which I refer to, as I have no further recollection. I can see that:
Wednesday the 31st of March 2004 there was a shortage of £101.95.
Wednesday 8th April 2004 a surplus of £19.38

9. From my recollection, I do not think that I would have been concerned by any
of the differences, shortages or surplus. I can say this, as whilst I was a Sub-
Postmistress, in broad terms, I used to consider a balance of less than £20
either way, acceptable, given the volumes of cash and transactions.

10. I cannot add anything further regarding the Horizon IT system that was in place
at the Marine Drive Post Office, to that, that is within my original statement made
19th October 2006. Specifically, in relation to the lottery error notices, I do not
recall any issues with Horizon. In relation to the shortage of £101.95, my
understanding and recollection is that this was pinpointed to the Monday of that
week and attributed to human error. I cannot remember anything further
regarding the computer IT freeze and reboot of the screen, than what is in my
original statement of 19th October 2006. I do not recall any further investigation
regarding these issues.
